# 🏦 Bank Customer Churn Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn in a retail banking environment using SQL and Power BI.

The objective is to identify the factors associated with customer attrition, quantify the financial impact of churn, and provide actionable business recommendations to improve customer retention.

The analysis follows a complete analytics workflow including data cleaning, feature engineering, KPI calculation, exploratory analysis, dashboard development, and business recommendations.

---

# 🎯 Business Problem

Customer churn is one of the largest challenges faced by retail banks.

Losing existing customers reduces long-term profitability and increases customer acquisition costs.

This project aims to answer key business questions such as:

- What is the overall customer churn rate?
- Which customers are most likely to leave?
- Which products have the highest churn?
- Which geographic markets require immediate attention?
- How much annual revenue is at risk?
- What actions can reduce customer attrition?

---

# 📊 Dataset

Dataset: Bank Customer Churn (Churn Modelling)

Records: 10,000 customers

Target Variable:

- Exited
  - 1 = Customer Left
  - 0 = Customer Stayed

Main Features:

- Credit Score
- Geography
- Gender
- Age
- Tenure
- Balance
- Number of Products
- Credit Card Status
- Active Membership
- Estimated Salary

# 📊 Key Findings

- Overall churn rate: **20.4%**
- Germany recorded the highest churn rate.
- Inactive customers churned 88% more than active customers.
- Customers aged 45–59 showed the highest churn rates.
- Credit score had relatively weak predictive power.
- Customers with three or more products experienced significantly higher churn.
- Approximately **$2.89M** in annual revenue is at risk from customer attrition.
